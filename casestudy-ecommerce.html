About the Client

A fast-growing e-commerce brand overwhelmed by customer support volume.

The client runs a direct-to-consumer e-commerce brand selling fitness supplements online. With a growing customer base of 15,000+ active buyers, their 3-person support team was handling 600–800 tickets per day — mostly repetitive questions about order status, shipping times, and returns policy.

🛍️

D2C E-Commerce Brand — Fitness Supplements

15,000+ active customers · 600–800 daily support tickets · 3-person support team at capacity · Goal: automate routine queries so the human team can focus on complex, high-value customer interactions.

Problem

A 3-person team drowning in 800 tickets a day — mostly the same questions.

Over 70% of support tickets were variations of the same four questions: "Where is my order?", "What's your return policy?", "Can I change my address?", and "Do you ship internationally?". The team was spending the majority of their day copy-pasting the same answers instead of handling genuine edge cases.

Response times were slipping. Customer satisfaction was dropping. And hiring more agents wasn't a scalable solution.

Solution

A GPT-4 powered chatbot trained on every product, policy, and FAQ.

Axythra built a custom AI chatbot using Voiceflow as the conversation design layer and GPT-4 as the intelligence engine. The bot was trained on the brand's full product catalog, shipping policies, return procedures, and common support scenarios. It integrates directly with the order management system to pull real-time order status for any customer.

For edge cases the AI can't confidently handle, it performs a clean handoff to a human agent with the full conversation context — so agents never have to ask a customer to repeat themselves.

Challenges & Problem-Solving

Getting the AI to know when it doesn't know.

The Challenge

The chatbot was confidently giving wrong answers

Early testing revealed the bot would sometimes hallucinate policy details or give outdated product information when asked questions outside its training scope. This was creating more frustration than the manual support it was replacing.

Root Cause

No confidence threshold or uncertainty detection in place

The initial prompt engineering didn't include explicit instructions for the model to express uncertainty or escalate when confidence was low. GPT-4 defaults to generating fluent, confident-sounding responses even when the answer is uncertain.

Resolution

Uncertainty detection layer and strict knowledge boundary prompting

The system prompt was restructured with explicit knowledge boundaries — the bot was instructed to only answer from its provided knowledge base and to escalate immediately if a query fell outside that scope. A confidence-detection layer was added to the Voiceflow flow that catches low-certainty responses before they reach the customer. Wrong answer rate dropped to under 1% after the fix.

Outcome

800+ queries handled daily. Support team finally breathing again.

The chatbot now handles over 85% of incoming support queries automatically. The human team handles only genuine edge cases, complex complaints, and relationship-building interactions. Response times dropped from 6–8 hours to under 2 minutes for all routine queries.
